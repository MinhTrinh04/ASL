ABSTRACT
The recent surge in distance education, catalyzed by the COVID-19 pandemic,
has highlighted its advantages in terms of flexibility and accessibility. However,
the predominant focus on theoretical instructions and the limitations in providing
practical content have hindered learning outcomes and student engagement. Fur-
thermore, over half of parents have reported that their children are not learning
adequately or at all when participating in online education, due to the lack of inter-
action in remote teaching methods and the absence of motivating learning content.
To address these challenges, this research investigates the integration of virtual re-
ality technology into practical lessons, aiming to enhance the quality of remote
practical education and foster independent learning skills in students.
In order to enhance interactive learning experiences, this project simulates a
specialized practical lesson in computer networking, where learners utilize a virtual
reality application to practice Ethernet network wiring, receiving realistic feedback
throughout the process.
Furthermore, a potential solution to enhance motivation in practical lessons in-
volves incorporating experiential learning through gamification. This project in-
troduces a virtual reality role-playing game focused on waste classification aware-
ness, addressing sustainable development goals outlined by the United Nations.
The game integrates generative artificial intelligence to facilitate interactive con-
versations and enhance the learning experience.
A comparative analysis between traditional methods and immersive VR expe-
riences, using the aforementioned lessons, reveals that integrating VR technology
with pedagogical approaches effectively addresses the need for interactive practice,
while significantly enhancing learner engagement and educational outcomes. This
project not only contributes to the advancement of remote practical education but
also unveils novel avenues for the development of educational technology.
